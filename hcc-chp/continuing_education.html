Continuing Education Requirements

The Center for Healthcare Professionals (CHP) maintains a database of current professional continuing education requirements for a variety of healthcare disciplines. Below are some of these requirements and references to the original source information. Each healthcare professional should keep up to date on the current requirements that pertain to their specific licensure/certification.

Healthcare Professional Regulatory Agency # of CEUs Required Frequency of Req'd CEUs
Clinical Laboratory Technician American Society of the Clinical Pathologists (ASCP) Board of Certification (BOC) Typically 36 points, varies with certifications Every 3 Years
Community Health Worker Texas Department of State Health Services 20 Every 2 Years
Dental Assistant, Registered & Certified Texas State Board of Dental Examiners 6 - 12 depending on credentials Every Year
Dental Hygienist, Licensed Texas State Board of Dental Examiners 12 Every Year
Health Information Technician, Registered Commission on Certification for Health Informatics and Information Management (CCHIIM) 20 - 50 depending on certification level Every 2 Years
Histologic Technician American Society of the Clinical Pathologists (ASCP) Board of Certification (BOC) Typically 36 point, varies with certifications Every 3 Years
Human Service Professional (Social Workers) Texas State Board of Social Work Examiners 30 Every 2 Years
Message Therapist, Registered Texas Department of State Health Services 12 Every 2 Years
Nurse, Registered & Vocational Texas Board of Nursing 20 Every 2 Years
Occupational Therapy Assistant, Certified Texas Board of Occupational Examiners 30 Every 2 Years
Pharmacy Technician, Certified Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education 20 Every 2 Years
Physical Therapist Assistant Texas Board of Physical Therapy Examiners 20 (PTA), 30 (PT) Every 2 Years
Radiologic Technologist, Registered Texas Department of State Health Services 24 Every 2 Years
Respiratory Therapist, Certified Texas Department of State Health Services 24 (for state certification), 30 (for CRT and RRT) 2 Years (for state certification), 5 Years (for CRT and RRT)
