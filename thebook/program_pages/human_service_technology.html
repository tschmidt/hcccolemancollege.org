Certificate Testing
Students completing the Associate Degree in Human Services program qualify to sit for the Board Certified Human Service Practitioner Certification.
  • Completing the Licensed Chemical Dependency and Counselor (LCDC) certificate program qualifies students to earn Counselor Intern (CI) status with the Department of State Health Services (DSHS), Substance Abuse Division. To acquire a Licensed Counselor Certification the student must:
    • Complete the AS in Human Services
    • Complete 4,000 hours of filed experience
    • Pass a written exam
  • Specific details on obtaining the LCDC certification can be found at the DSHS website.
  • Certified Prevention Specialist MSA Certificate program qualifies individuals to become a Certified Prevention Intern. A written exam and 2,000 hours are required before becoming a Certified Prevention Specialist.
  • Community Health Workers MSA Certificate program qualifies students to become certified Community Health Workers with the Department of State Health Services. No exam required.
Employment Opportunities
Graduates may find employment in drug and alcohol abuse counseling settings, correctional institutions, mental health centers, social service agencies, group homes and various community agencies.
Average Salary
Annual, entry-level salaries vary between $24,000 and $32,000.

Community Health Worker
The Community Health Worker program at HCC Coleman admits students to the program twice a year, fall and spring. Students who complete the Community Health Worker Marketable Skills Award receive CHW certification from the Texas Department of State Health Services (DSHS). The curriculum is built upon educational principles for adult learners and operates using participatory and experiential learning principles.
The CHW program uses a variety of community-based programs as placement sites to allow students to practice in a real life setting the principles and practice of CHWs. For a current list of sites, contact the instructor during the course work. New sites are being added each semester.

We are thrilled you want to start your career in health care at HCC Coleman College

However, unlike other HCC colleges, enrolling at HCC Coleman is a competitive and selective process. Some programs require successful completion of courses before you are allowed to apply for admission. Not all programs admit all the applicants they receive. Additionally, some programs accept one class per year, while others admit two.

You can see there are a lot of details involved in choosing the best program for you. That's why we want to get to know you better and ask you to visit our campus in person prior to applying for admission.

But there is on common element to every student's application: you must be a Houston Community College student at the time you apply for admission to an HCC Coleman College health science program. Find the application to HCC at coleman.hccs.edu/about-us/how-to-apply/.

Once you have completed the HCC application, or if you already have an HCC student identification number, visit coleman.hccs.edu to begin your health science application process. If you have questions, contact us at studentsfirst@hccs.edu or chat online with an HCC Coleman representative at askColeman.
